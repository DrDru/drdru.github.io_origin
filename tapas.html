寒さむくて死しにそう
Play audio

寒さむい cold
寒さむくて is the て-form of 寒さむい. て-form has several uses. In this case it allows to link 寒さむい to what's next.
死しぬ to die
死しに Dying
そう to have the appearance
死しにそう about to die

This expression often is often used in conversation and could be translated as :

> "I'm so cold I could die" or "I'm freezing to death".

あそこに行いこう
Play audio

あそこ there
に indicates direction
行いく to go
行いこう is the volitional form of 行いく which turns "to go" into "let's go".

> Let's go there

あー aah (onomatopeia)

撃うつのはやめて
Play audio

撃うつ to shoot
止やめる to stop

Appending のは transforms the verb 撃うつ into a noun so it becomes 'shooting' or 'the act of shooting'
やめて is the て-form of 止やめる which in this case is used to express a command. It's rather soft compared with やめろ which would be direct / rude.

"Stop the act of shooting"

> Don't shoot

武器ぶきを使つかうのは良よくない
Play audio

武器ぶき weapon
使つか to use
良よく good
ない is the negation so 良よくない is 'not good'

武器ぶきを使つかう to use the weapon
Here again のは transforms the proposition into a noun.
"The act of using this weapon is not good."

> Using this weapon would be bad

少すこ休やすみたいだけなの
Play audio

少すこし a little
休やすむ to rest
休やすみたい I want to rest
だけ just

少すこし休やすみたいだけ could be translated as :

> We just want to rest a bit.

Adding なの at the end softens the sentence and make it explanatory. The idea could be expressed in English by saying "It's that we just want to rest a bit".

暖かくして休まないともう何日も寝てない

暖かい warm

もう means 'more' or 'again'. It emphasizes she is tired of this.

寝る to sleep

寝てない makes the continuous form into a negation



２、３時間経ったら起こして

時 hour
時間 hour with the conotation of the duration
経つ is to pass. It's used to when talking about the time that passes.

起こす to wake up
起こして is the て-form of 起こす. It expresses a command in a soft way.
> When 2, 3 hours pass, wake me up

or more naturally
> Wake me up after a few hours.
