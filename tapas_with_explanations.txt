寒くて死にそう 

寒い cold  
寒くて is the て-form of 寒い. て-form has several uses. In this case it allows to link 寒い to what's next.
死ぬ to die 
死に Dying
そう to have the appearance  
死にそう about to die


This first sentence contains two N5 words but the way they are combined makes it a bit complicated.
We have cold 寒い and death 死. From the image we can see she's cold so it's not too hard to infer the global meaning of this sentence.  
Altogether it means "It has the appearance that I'm dying of cold".
This expression is often used in conversation and could be translated as :

> "I'm so cold I could die" or "I'm freezing to death".

撃つのはやめて

撃つ to shoot
止める  to stop

Appending のは transforms the verb 撃つ into a noun so it becomes 'shooting' or 'the act of shooting'
やめて is the て-form of 止める which in this case is used to express a command. It's rather soft compared with やめろ which is very rude.

"Stop the act of shooting" 

> Don't shoot 

For instance 
あそこに行くのはやめて
would be 'stop the act of going there' or more naturally "Don't go there"

武器を使う to use the weapon

"Using this weapon is not good"
